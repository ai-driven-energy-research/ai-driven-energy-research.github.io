Research Integrity

Originality

Submissions must be original work not previously published or under consideration at another venue. Preprints (arXiv, SSRN, etc.) are permitted and do not constitute prior publication.

Data Integrity

  • Data must not be fabricated or falsified
  • Data processing and cleaning steps must be documented in the process log
  • If data has been modified from its raw form, the raw data and transformation scripts must be provided

Authorship

All listed authors must have made a substantive contribution to the research. AI tools are not listed as authors. The use of AI tools is disclosed in the process log and AI disclosure section, not in the author list.

Conflicts of Interest

Authors must disclose any financial or personal relationships that could influence the research. Reviewers must recuse themselves from submissions where they have a conflict of interest. Editors will not handle submissions from their own research groups.

AI Disclosure Policy

AIDER's position on AI in research is clear: using AI is encouraged, hiding it is not.

What Must Be Disclosed

  • Any AI tool used at any stage of the research — writing, coding, analysis, literature review, figure generation, data processing
  • The specific tool and version (e.g., "Claude 3.5 Sonnet via Anthropic API", "GPT-4 via ChatGPT", "GitHub Copilot")
  • How the tool was used (e.g., "code generation for data preprocessing pipeline", "drafting the methodology section", "generating initial literature search queries")
  • The extent of human modification of AI-generated content

Where to Disclose

  • Process log: Full AI session transcripts in process-log/ai-sessions/
  • Submission form: Summary of AI tools used in the "AI Tools Used" field
  • Manuscript: Optional acknowledgement section

What Happens If AI Use Is Not Disclosed

If evidence of undisclosed AI use is found during review, authors will be asked to update their disclosure. If the omission is judged to be deliberate concealment, the submission will be rejected. For published papers, a correction notice will be issued.

AI in Peer Review

Reviewers may use AI tools to assist their reviews. This must be disclosed at the end of the review. The editorial decision remains with human editors.

Open Access Policy

AIDER is a diamond open-access journal:

  • No article processing charges (APCs)
  • No subscription fees
  • No embargo periods
  • All content is immediately and permanently available
  • Papers are licensed CC-BY 4.0; code is MIT or Apache 2.0; data is CC0 or CC-BY 4.0

Corrections and Retractions

Corrections

Since AIDER papers are living GitHub repositories, corrections are handled as pull requests. Minor corrections (typos, clarifications) can be merged by the authors. Substantive corrections (methodology changes, result updates) require editor approval and a version note in the repository.

Retractions

In cases of serious misconduct (data fabrication, plagiarism, undisclosed conflicts), the editorial board may retract a paper. Retracted papers remain in the repository with a prominent retraction notice. The retraction reason is publicly documented.

Complaints and Appeals

Authors may appeal editorial decisions by commenting on their submission issue with additional evidence or arguments. Appeals are reviewed by an editor not involved in the original decision. For complaints about editorial conduct, contact the founding editor at jianou.jiang@eng.ox.ac.uk.

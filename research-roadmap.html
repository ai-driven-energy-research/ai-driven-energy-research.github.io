About This Roadmap

This roadmap is maintained by the AIDER editorial committee and updated quarterly. It maps the landscape of AI + energy research — what has been done, where the gaps are, and what needs work.

This is not an exclusive list. Unlisted topics are welcome — if your research falls within AIDER's scope, we want to see it. The roadmap exists to help researchers identify high-impact opportunities, not to limit what gets published.

Last updated: Q1 2026

Research Areas at a Glance

Well-Covered

ML for Renewable Energy Forecasting

Wind, solar, and load forecasting using neural networks, gradient boosting, and hybrid models. Large body of existing literature.

Well-Covered

AI for Power Grid Optimisation

Reinforcement learning and optimisation for dispatch, demand response, and grid stability. Active research community.

Active

Physics-Informed Neural Networks for Energy

PINNs applied to fluid dynamics, heat transfer, and structural analysis in energy systems. Growing rapidly but reproducibility is poor.

Active

ML-Augmented CFD for Energy

Surrogate models, turbulence closures, and mesh-free methods for wind turbines, combustion, and thermal systems.

Active

AI for Building Energy Management

HVAC control, occupancy prediction, retrofit optimisation. Practical applications exist but benchmarks are fragmented.

Active

Generative Models for Materials Discovery

Diffusion models and VAEs for battery materials, catalysts, and photovoltaics. Fast-moving but limited to a few research groups.

Emerging

LLM-Assisted Research Workflows

Using large language models to design experiments, write code, and accelerate the research process itself. Almost no published work with reproducible process logs.

Emerging

Foundation Models for Energy Systems

Pre-trained models for time series, sensor data, and multi-physics. No established benchmarks. Major opportunity.

Emerging

AI for Energy Policy Analysis

NLP for regulatory text, scenario modelling with AI, automated policy impact assessment. Very little published work.

Emerging

Multi-Agent Systems for Energy

Coordinated AI agents for grid management, market simulation, and distributed energy resources. Theoretical work exists but practical studies are rare.

Detailed Gap Analysis

Well-Covered ML for Renewable Energy Forecasting

What exists

  • Thousands of papers on wind speed/power forecasting using LSTMs, CNNs, Transformers
  • Solar irradiance prediction with satellite imagery and ground sensors
  • Hybrid models combining NWP with ML post-processing
  • Probabilistic forecasting with quantile regression and ensemble methods

What is missing

  • Standardised benchmarks — most papers use private datasets, making comparison impossible
  • Reproducible code — fewer than 10% of forecasting papers publish working code
  • Multi-site and transfer learning studies at scale
  • Integration of forecasting uncertainty into downstream decision-making

Suggested research questions

  • Can a single pre-trained forecasting model generalise across wind farms without fine-tuning?
  • What is the actual value of deep learning over gradient boosting when both are properly tuned?
  • How should forecast uncertainty propagate into energy trading or grid dispatch decisions?
Well-Covered AI for Power Grid Optimisation

What exists

  • RL for real-time dispatch and demand response
  • Graph neural networks for power flow analysis
  • ML-based fault detection and predictive maintenance
  • Optimisation of EV charging and battery storage scheduling

What is missing

  • Real-world deployment studies (most work is simulation-only)
  • Safety guarantees for RL-based control in critical infrastructure
  • Open grid simulation environments with realistic dynamics
  • Studies at distribution level (most focus on transmission)

Suggested research questions

  • Can safe RL maintain grid stability guarantees while optimising for cost?
  • What is the gap between simulation-trained agents and real-world deployment?
  • How should heterogeneous DERs be coordinated at the distribution level?
Active Physics-Informed Neural Networks for Energy

What exists

  • PINNs for solving forward and inverse problems in fluid mechanics
  • Applications to heat transfer, structural mechanics, and electromagnetics
  • Several benchmark problems (Navier-Stokes, Burgers equation)

What is missing

  • Reproducible PINN implementations for real energy systems (not toy problems)
  • Fair comparison with traditional solvers on computation time and accuracy
  • PINNs for turbulent flows at realistic Reynolds numbers
  • Multi-physics coupling (thermal-structural, fluid-structure)

Suggested research questions

  • Under what conditions do PINNs outperform classical numerical methods for energy applications?
  • Can PINNs be used for real-time digital twins of wind turbines or heat exchangers?
  • How do training strategies affect PINN convergence for stiff multi-physics problems?
Active ML-Augmented CFD for Energy

What exists

  • Neural network surrogate models for aerodynamic design
  • ML turbulence closures (learned Reynolds stress models)
  • Graph-based and mesh-free neural operators (DeepONet, FNO)
  • Data-driven reduced-order models for parametric studies

What is missing

  • Open datasets of high-fidelity CFD results for training
  • Studies on generalisation — do ML models trained on one geometry transfer to others?
  • Uncertainty quantification for ML-augmented CFD predictions
  • End-to-end workflows combining ML with traditional solvers

Suggested research questions

  • Can neural operators replace mesh generation for complex energy system geometries?
  • What training data volume is needed for reliable ML turbulence models?
  • How should ML-CFD uncertainty be communicated to engineering decision-makers?
Active AI for Building Energy Management

What exists

  • RL-based HVAC control showing 15-30% energy savings in simulation
  • Occupancy prediction using sensor fusion and computer vision
  • Building energy modelling with neural networks (EnergyPlus surrogate models)

What is missing

  • Standardised building simulation benchmarks for RL comparison
  • Long-term field studies (beyond lab/simulation environments)
  • Scalable approaches that work across building types without re-training
  • Integration of comfort, energy, and cost objectives with formal guarantees

Suggested research questions

  • Can a pre-trained RL controller transfer across buildings with minimal adaptation?
  • What is the real-world energy saving gap between simulation and deployment?
  • How should occupant comfort constraints be learned rather than hard-coded?
Active Generative Models for Materials Discovery

What exists

  • VAEs and diffusion models for crystal structure generation
  • Graph neural networks for molecular property prediction
  • Active learning for high-throughput screening of battery materials

What is missing

  • Experimental validation of AI-discovered materials (most work is computational)
  • Open datasets linking computational predictions to experimental outcomes
  • Synthesisability constraints in generative models
  • Standardised evaluation metrics for generated materials

Suggested research questions

  • What fraction of AI-generated candidate materials are actually synthesisable?
  • Can generative models incorporate manufacturing constraints end-to-end?
  • How should the search space be bounded to produce practically useful materials?
Emerging LLM-Assisted Research Workflows

What exists

  • Anecdotal reports and blog posts on using LLMs for research
  • A few papers on AI-assisted literature review and hypothesis generation
  • Tools like Claude Code, Aider, and Copilot used widely but rarely documented

What is missing

  • Formal studies on how LLM-assisted research compares to traditional workflows in quality and speed
  • Reproducible process logs showing the full AI-human interaction during research
  • Best practices for prompt engineering in scientific contexts
  • Evaluation of AI agent reliability for different research tasks

Suggested research questions

  • Does LLM-assisted coding produce more or fewer bugs than manual coding in research contexts?
  • How does the quality of AI-assisted literature reviews compare to manual reviews?
  • What is the optimal human-AI task division for different types of energy research?
Emerging Foundation Models for Energy Systems

What exists

  • General-purpose time series foundation models (TimesFM, Chronos) with limited energy domain testing
  • Pre-trained models for weather/climate (Pangu-Weather, FourCastNet)
  • Small-scale transfer learning studies between energy datasets

What is missing

  • Energy-specific foundation models trained on diverse operational data
  • Benchmark datasets spanning multiple energy sub-domains
  • Studies on when fine-tuning a foundation model beats training from scratch
  • Multi-modal models that combine sensor data, images, and text

Suggested research questions

  • Can a single foundation model serve wind forecasting, load prediction, and fault detection?
  • What pre-training data composition produces the best energy domain generalisation?
  • How do energy foundation models handle distribution shift from climate change?
Emerging AI for Energy Policy Analysis

What exists

  • NLP for extracting structured data from energy regulations
  • Agent-based models for energy market simulation
  • A few studies using LLMs to summarise policy documents

What is missing

  • Automated policy impact assessment tools
  • Cross-country comparative analysis using NLP at scale
  • Integration of energy system models with policy scenario generators
  • Reproducible policy analysis pipelines

Suggested research questions

  • Can LLMs reliably extract compliance requirements from energy regulations?
  • How can AI scenario modelling inform carbon pricing policy design?
  • What is the accuracy of AI-generated policy summaries compared to expert analysis?
Emerging Multi-Agent Systems for Energy

What exists

  • Theoretical frameworks for multi-agent reinforcement learning in power systems
  • Small-scale simulations of peer-to-peer energy trading
  • Game-theoretic models for competitive energy markets

What is missing

  • Scalable multi-agent simulations with realistic grid dynamics
  • Coordination protocols for heterogeneous agents (EVs, batteries, solar, wind)
  • Real-world pilot studies of AI-coordinated microgrids
  • Communication-efficient multi-agent algorithms for bandwidth-limited settings

Suggested research questions

  • Can decentralised multi-agent control match centralised optimisation for microgrid management?
  • How should agents handle adversarial behaviour in peer-to-peer energy markets?
  • What communication topology minimises coordination overhead while maintaining performance?

Cross-Cutting Gaps

These gaps span multiple research areas and represent structural challenges in the field:

Reproducibility tooling Most AI + energy papers lack working code. We need standardised reproducibility frameworks — containerised environments, automated testing, and benchmark suites that make it easy to reproduce and compare results.
Benchmark datasets The field lacks shared, high-quality benchmark datasets. Most researchers use private data, making fair comparison impossible. We need open datasets with standardised train/test splits, preprocessing pipelines, and evaluation metrics.
Multi-physics coupling Energy systems involve coupled physics — thermal, structural, fluid, electrical. Most ML work addresses each domain in isolation. Methods for learning coupled multi-physics systems are largely unexplored.
Uncertainty quantification AI predictions in energy systems need calibrated uncertainty estimates to support engineering decisions. Most papers report point predictions only. Conformal prediction, Bayesian methods, and ensemble approaches are underused.
Real-world deployment studies The gap between simulation and deployment is vast. We need more studies that test AI methods on real hardware, real data, and real constraints — not just cleaner versions of the same simulation environments.

How to Claim a Gap

See a gap that matches your expertise? Here's how to start:

  1. Fork the template. Create your repository from the AIDER paper template.
  2. Reference the gap in your submission. When you open a submission issue, mention which roadmap gap your work addresses. This helps editors assign appropriate reviewers.
  3. Work collaboratively. Many gaps are best addressed by teams. See the Collaborative Research Guide for how to set up a team project.

You don't need to "reserve" a gap. Multiple teams can work on the same topic — different approaches to the same problem are valuable.

Suggest a Gap

If you see a research gap that isn't listed here, we want to know about it:

  • GitHub issue: Open an issue on the website repository with the title "Roadmap suggestion: [your topic]"
  • Email: Send a brief description to jianou.jiang@eng.ox.ac.uk

Include: the research area, what exists, what's missing, and why it matters. The editorial committee reviews suggestions quarterly.

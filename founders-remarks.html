"The future is not something that happens to you, not something you wait for. The future is what you imagine and fight for — it is what you make happen." — Dante, Divine Comedy

We founded this journal because we believe that the smallest beginning, honestly made, can grow into something that changes the world — and that the world only changes when someone decides to stop waiting.

The Moment We Are In

Humanity stands at a point of historical fracture. Empires are exhausting themselves through overextension. Institutions are collapsing under the weight of their own broken promises. The climate is being pushed past boundaries we once thought permanent. And energy — the foundation upon which all of civilisation runs — stands at a crossroads unlike any before it.

Into this moment arrives a tool whose name has been mythologised, inflated, and profoundly misunderstood. We call it "artificial intelligence." Its true nature is more precise, and more humble: supervised machine learning — a methodology of infinite iteration, constantly approaching truth. Its power lies not in being a god, but in being a mirror: it reflects the data we give it, the frameworks we impose, and the questions we choose to ask.

This is precisely why it must be open.

Against Enclosure

Knowledge, once monopolised, becomes an instrument of power. Throughout history, every elite class has sought to control the narrative — to determine what can be believed, what can be proven, what can be published. Scientists publish papers while their code sits locked on a hard drive. Conclusions are announced while processes remain hidden. AI is used, and its footprints are quietly erased. This is not science. It is another form of elite storytelling.

AIDER refuses this.

We believe the value of a paper lies not only in its conclusions, but in the complete journey toward them — every line of code, every AI intervention, every human decision, every intermediate result. We require all of it to be public, executable, and reproducible. We charge authors nothing. We charge readers nothing. Because knowledge was never meant to have a cover price. We publish on GitHub, because research should not end at publication — it should begin there.

This is not merely an academic choice. It is a civilisational one.

An Honest Warning

But we must pause here, and say something less stirring.

A mentor we deeply respect, upon seeing this project, offered us a warning we believe deserves to be preserved here in full — not buried in an internal memo:

"Academic journals are far more complicated than they appear. Once government funding, corporate partnerships, and resource allocation enter the picture, the web of interests becomes extraordinarily tangled, and the challenge of governance far exceeds the challenge of technology. Getting the early process right is one thing. Sustaining credibility and managing conflict over the long term is quite another. Of the non-profit organisations I have worked with, I would say 95% did not survive."

He is right.

We cannot argue with this. The historical evidence is on his side. The mathematician and historian Peter Turchin showed us, through quantitative modelling of civilisations across centuries, that institutions almost never die from external attack. They die from internal erosion — when too many actors compete for too little power, when the interests of those inside the institution begin to diverge from the purpose the institution was built to serve. A non-profit journal, once it touches government grants, corporate sponsorship, and academic prestige, faces exactly this gravitational pull. We have no reason to believe we are immune to it.

Why Begin at All?

Because we believe that clearly seeing the likelihood of failure is itself the first act of resistance against it. Most of the 95% did not die because their ideals were too high. They died because they never honestly confronted the roots of their own fragility — because they assumed governance problems would resolve themselves, that conflicts of interest would not materialise, that credibility could be sustained on goodwill alone.

We refuse to pretend.

This is why transparency is not a feature we have added to AIDER — it is the foundation on which the entire structure is built. Not only transparency in research, but operational transparency: all editorial decisions documented, all reviewer processes public, all AI tool usage logged and auditable, all conflicts of interest mandatorily disclosed. We know these mechanisms cannot fully prevent corruption, just as democratic institutions cannot fully prevent abuse of power. But they make corruption visible. And visibility is the precondition for accountability. Accountability is the precondition for self-correction.

We also admit, honestly, that we do not know how far we will go. Perhaps in five years we will have joined the 95%. But we would rather fail publicly, with a complete record intact — so that the next person who tries this knows where we fell, and why, and can walk further than we did.

Because that, in itself, is the spirit of science.

Energy as Battleground

Energy is the most urgent battleground of this era. Without clean, stable, and equitably distributed energy, every vision of a better world collapses before it begins. AI-driven energy research stands precisely at the intersection of the two most consequential transformations of our time. But if the knowledge produced in this field remains locked behind publication fees, subscription walls, and black-box algorithms, we are simply playing the old game with new tools.

Asimov, in Foundation, dreamed of psychohistory — a science capable of predicting and guiding the course of human civilisation. He understood that such a system, to truly serve humanity, would have to meet one non-negotiable condition: it must be completely open and transparent, beyond the interest of any single nation or power bloc, speaking for all of humanity rather than for any one empire. Without that condition, it would become merely another instrument of oppression dressed in the language of progress.

AIDER's ambition is far smaller than psychohistory, and far more concrete. But it follows the same logic: a genuinely open knowledge platform is the most powerful tool we have against civilisational entropy. Not because openness solves every problem, but because closure inevitably creates more problems than it solves.

A Covenant

The team that founded AIDER comes from a variety of backgrounds — spanning academia and industry, East and West. We are united by one conviction:

Open knowledge is the most powerful weapon against civilisational decline.

We remember that Homer wrote when society was collapsing. Dante wrote when society was collapsing. It was in the cracks of those collapses that the greatest works were born, and the deepest truths were spoken. Perhaps we are living in such a moment.

And so we choose to begin now, rather than wait for a safer time. We choose openness over enclosure. We choose transparency over mystique. We choose to leave these founding remarks — including the mentor's warning, and this honest admission of our own fragility — permanently in this place, as a covenant with ourselves and with everyone who joins us.

We know how hard this road is.

We choose to walk it anyway.

The future is not waited for. It is imagined, fought for, built line by line, paper by paper, one open peer review at a time.

This is what we are doing. We invite you to join us.

Jianou Jiang

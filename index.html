Founding Principles

AIDER is built on five commitments that define every aspect of the journal.

1
Open process, not just open access We publish the complete research pipeline — every AI agent session, every human decision, every intermediate result. The process of discovery is as valuable as the outcome.
2
AI is a collaborator, not a secret Using AI to write, code, analyse, and review is expected and must be documented. Research produced with AI agents is first-class scholarship here.
3
If it can't be executed, it doesn't count Every paper must include code that runs, data that downloads, and a script that regenerates the published results. Automated verification is part of the review.
4
A paper is a living repository Published work lives on GitHub. It can be forked, extended, corrected, and built upon by anyone. Research does not end at publication — it begins there.
5
Zero barriers to entry No publication fees. No subscription fees. No institutional access required. Diamond open access — sustained by the goodwill of those who believe open science should stay open. Readers never pay. Authors never pay.

Submission Requirements

Every submission to AIDER is a complete, executable research package — not just a PDF.

  • Required
    Paper manuscript — LaTeX or Markdown, submitted as source
  • Required
    Code repository — all source code, with dependency management, setup instructions, and a working entry point
  • Required
    Data — in-repo or hosted on Zenodo / HuggingFace with a persistent DOI
  • Required
    Research process log — AI agent transcripts, session logs, prompts, and a timestamped record of all human decisions and interventions
  • Required
    Reproducibility script — a single command (bash reproduce.sh) that regenerates all published figures and tables

Submission Pipeline

Create repository from template Fork paper-template and populate with your manuscript, code, data, and process log
Submit via GitHub Issue Open a submission issue with your repository link and completed checklist
Automated verification CI pipeline clones your repo, installs dependencies, and executes reproduce.sh to validate reproducibility
AI-powered review pipeline AI agents pre-screen the paper, verify bold claims, and audit reference accessibility. An editor reviews the AI reports and makes the final decision
Publication Accepted repository is archived in the AIDER GitHub organisation with a Zenodo DOI

Automated Review, Evaluation & Correction

AIDER aims to make the full review lifecycle — from initial assessment to post-publication correction — as automated as possible. Human editors supervise; AI agents do the heavy lifting.

Review

Automated Assessment

AI agents evaluate submissions for methodological rigour, statistical validity, code quality, and consistency between claims and results.

Verify

Automated Verification

CI pipelines clone the repository, install dependencies, execute reproduce.sh, and compare generated outputs against published figures and tables.

Correct

Continuous Correction

After publication, automated checks detect dependency breakage, data drift, and newly reported issues. Corrections are proposed as pull requests on the living repository.

Transparency

All reviews — automated and human — are published alongside the paper. Reviewer identities are disclosed. AI review agents' prompts and outputs are versioned and publicly available. Nothing is hidden.

Human Oversight

Editors make all accept/reject decisions. Automated review augments human judgement — it does not replace editorial responsibility. Authors may respond to and dispute any automated finding.

Research Gaps & Opportunities

The AIDER editorial committee maintains a living roadmap of research gaps at the AI + energy intersection. Here are three high-impact opportunities.

Emerging

Foundation Models for Energy Systems

Pre-trained models for time series, sensor data, and multi-physics. No established benchmarks exist. A single foundation model serving wind forecasting, load prediction, and fault detection remains untested.

Emerging

LLM-Assisted Research Workflows

Researchers are using AI agents to write code and run experiments, but almost no published work documents the full AI-human interaction with reproducible process logs.

Active Gap

Reproducibility Tooling & Benchmarks

Most AI + energy papers lack working code. The field needs standardised benchmark datasets, containerised environments, and shared evaluation metrics.
